1. What Are Facebook Reels?

Facebook Reels are short-form vertical videos that can range from a few seconds up to 90 seconds in length. They are designed to be watched in a full-screen, immersive format, similar to the experience popularized by TikTok and later adopted by Instagram. Reels can include music, audio clips, voiceovers, text overlays, stickers, and a wide variety of visual effects that allow creators to express themselves in creative and engaging ways.

Unlike standard Facebook videos, which are typically horizontal and shared on a user's timeline or page, Reels are optimized for vertical viewing on mobile devices. They appear in a dedicated Reels feed, in the main News Feed, and can also be discovered through Facebook Watch and the Reels tab found on both personal profiles and Facebook Pages.

Reels were first introduced on Instagram in 2020 as a direct response to the explosive growth of TikTok. Facebook then began testing its own Reels feature in 2021 before rolling it out globally in 2022. Since then, Meta — the parent company of both Facebook and Instagram — has invested heavily in Reels, making it a cornerstone of its broader short-video strategy across all its platforms.

2. Why Facebook Reels Matter in 2026

The rise of short-form video is not a passing trend. People's attention spans are shorter, mobile usage is higher than ever, and the appetite for quick, entertaining content has never been greater.

For Facebook specifically, Reels represent a critical tool for re-engaging younger audiences who had been drifting toward TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Snapchat. By integrating Reels deeply into the Facebook experience — from the main feed to dedicated tabs and even in Facebook Groups — Meta has made it clear that short-form video is at the heart of its future plans.

For creators and businesses, this shift creates enormous opportunity. The Reels algorithm is designed to surface content to people who do not already follow the creator, which means that a single well-made Reel can reach hundreds of thousands or even millions of people who have never heard of your brand, page, or profile. This kind of organic reach is increasingly rare on Facebook, where the News Feed algorithm has historically favored content from friends and family over pages and public figures.

3. How to Watch Facebook Reels

Watching Reels on Facebook is simple and intuitive. There are several ways to access Reels content depending on how you prefer to browse:

3.1 The Reels Tab

On the Facebook mobile app, you will find a dedicated Reels tab in the navigation bar at the bottom of the screen. Tapping this tab opens a full-screen vertical video feed that plays Reels one after another, automatically. You can swipe up to move to the next Reel or swipe down to go back to a previous one. This experience is designed to be as frictionless as possible, keeping you in the flow of content without interruption.

3.2 The News Feed

Reels also appear directly in your main News Feed, mixed in with posts from friends, family, and pages you follow. Facebook's algorithm determines which Reels to show you based on your past behavior, including videos you have watched, liked, commented on, and shared. The more you engage with Reels content, the more accurately the algorithm can predict what you want to see next.

3.3 Facebook Watch

Facebook Watch is the platform's dedicated video hub, where you can find longer videos, live streams, and Reels all in one place. You can browse Reels by topic, follow specific creators, or let the algorithm serve you a personalized selection of content based on your interests.

3.4 On Profiles and Pages

Individual Facebook profiles and Pages now have a dedicated Reels section where you can browse all the Reels a particular creator or brand has published. This makes it easy to binge-watch content from a creator you enjoy or to quickly assess what kind of Reels a brand is producing before deciding whether to follow them.

4. How to Create Facebook Reels

Creating a Reel on Facebook is straightforward, even for beginners. Here is a step-by-step overview of the process:

4.1 Opening the Reels Creator

To create a Reel, open the Facebook app on your mobile device and tap the "Reels" option, which may appear at the top of your News Feed or in the Reels tab. From there, tap the camera icon or the "Create" button to open the Reels camera interface.

4.2 Recording or Uploading Video

You have two main options for creating a Reel: recording directly within the Facebook app using your phone's camera, or uploading a video clip that you have already recorded and saved to your device. Both options are valid, and many experienced creators prefer to shoot and edit their footage in a separate video editing app before uploading to Facebook for maximum quality and control.

If you choose to record within the app, you can record multiple clips by tapping and holding the record button, then releasing it to stop. Each clip is added to a sequence, and you can record as many clips as you need until you reach the maximum duration of 90 seconds.

4.3 Adding Music and Audio

One of the most powerful features of Facebook Reels is the ability to add music or audio clips from a vast library of licensed tracks. You can search for specific songs by title or artist, browse curated playlists, or select from trending audio clips that are currently popular on the platform.
